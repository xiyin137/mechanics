# Course Map

## Core And Extended Routes

The core route is the classroom spine: Lagrangian mechanics, Hamiltonian phase
space, Liouville tori, Kepler motion, the Euler top, resonant perturbation,
KAM survival and breakdown of invariant tori, three-body reduction,
CR3BP/Jacobi geometry, finite strain, linear elasticity, the first
gauge-theory examples, Navier-Stokes derivation, and exact laminar solutions.

The extended/project route adds the longer KAM proof sketch, cantori and
turnstiles, three-body ejection statistics, detailed elastic boundary-value
formulas, Shapere-Wilczek holonomy, Cosserat media, defect geometry,
Orr-Sommerfeld stability, and point-vortex Hamiltonian examples.

## Part I: Variational And Hamiltonian Mechanics

1. Configuration spaces and generalized coordinates.
2. Action principles and Euler-Lagrange equations.
3. Constraints, multipliers, and generalized forces.
4. Symmetry and Noether's theorem.
5. Legendre transform and Hamilton's equations.
6. Phase space, symplectic form, and Poisson brackets.
7. Canonical transformations and generating functions.

## Part II: Integrability And Rigid Bodies

8. Constants of motion and involution.
9. Liouville integrability and invariant tori.
10. Action integrals and angle variables.
11. Kepler problem as an integrable baseline.
12. Euler top and 3D rigid-body motion.

## Part III: Perturbation And Chaos

13. Near-integrable Hamiltonians.
14. Canonical perturbation theory and small divisors.
15. Resonance and Diophantine conditions.
16. KAM theorem and survival of invariant tori.
17. Breakdown of invariant tori, resonance overlap, and cantori.
18. Standard map and area-preserving maps.
19. Separatrices and chaotic layers.

## Part IV: Three-Body Dynamics

20. Newtonian three-body equations and conserved quantities.
21. Center-of-mass reduction, Jacobi coordinates, and Lagrange-Jacobi identity.
22. Central configurations and special rotating solutions.
23. Restricted three-body problem.
24. Jacobi integral, zero-velocity curves, and Lagrange points.
25. Asteroid-belt ejection probability experiment.

## Part V: Elastic And Deforming Bodies

26. Deformation maps and strain.
27. Finite elasticity, frame indifference, and compatibility.
28. Linear elasticity, elastic waves, bars, shafts, and beams.
29. Shape space, mechanical connection, and Shapere-Wilczek holonomy.
30. Local frames, Cosserat rods, and media.
31. Defects, curvature, and torsion.

## Part VI: Fluids

32. Material versus spatial descriptions.
33. Balance laws, Newtonian stress, and Navier-Stokes.
34. Exact Navier-Stokes solutions and laminar stability.
35. Ideal fluids, vorticity, and circulation.
36. Hamiltonian point vortices.
