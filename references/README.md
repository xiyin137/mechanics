# Reference Shelf

This folder is reserved for bibliographies, reading paths, and short annotated
reference notes.

This folder should contain only public-facing reference material. Private
planning notes, extraction ledgers, and machine-local source paths are
intentionally ignored by Git.

## Core Mechanics And Geometry

- V. I. Arnold, *Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics*.
- H. Goldstein, C. Poole, and J. Safko, *Classical Mechanics*.
- R. Abraham and J. E. Marsden, *Foundations of Mechanics*.
- J. E. Marsden and T. S. Ratiu, *Introduction to Mechanics and Symmetry*.
- R. Abraham, J. E. Marsden, and T. Ratiu, *Manifolds, Tensor Analysis, and
  Applications*.

## Integrability, Perturbation, And Chaos

- V. I. Arnold, V. V. Kozlov, and A. I. Neishtadt, *Mathematical Aspects of
  Classical and Celestial Mechanics*.
- A. J. Lichtenberg and M. A. Lieberman, *Regular and Chaotic Dynamics*.
- J. M. T. Thompson and H. B. Stewart, *Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos*.
- A. N. Kolmogorov, "On conservation of conditionally periodic motions for a
  small change in Hamilton's function," Dokl. Akad. Nauk SSSR 98 (1954).
- J. Moser, "On invariant curves of area-preserving mappings of an annulus,"
  *Nachrichten der Akademie der Wissenschaften in Gottingen* (1962).
- V. I. Arnold, "Proof of a theorem of A. N. Kolmogorov on the invariance of
  quasi-periodic motions under small perturbations of the Hamiltonian,"
  *Russian Mathematical Surveys* 18 (1963).

## Celestial And Three-Body Mechanics

- C. D. Murray and S. F. Dermott, *Solar System Dynamics*.
- V. Szebehely, *Theory of Orbits: The Restricted Problem of Three Bodies*.
- M. Valtonen and H. Karttunen, *The Three-Body Problem*.
- D. Heggie and P. Hut, *The Gravitational Million-Body Problem*.
- Y. Kozai, "Secular perturbations of asteroids with high inclination and
  eccentricity," *Astronomical Journal* 67 (1962), 591.
- M. L. Lidov, "The evolution of orbits of artificial satellites of planets
  under the action of gravitational perturbations of external bodies,"
  *Planetary and Space Science* 9 (1962), 719--759.
- J. Wisdom, "The origin of the Kirkwood gaps: a mapping for asteroidal
  motion near the 3/1 commensurability," *Astronomical Journal* 87 (1982),
  577--593.

## Continua, Deformation, And Fluids

- C. Truesdell and W. Noll, *The Non-Linear Field Theories of Mechanics*.
- J. E. Marsden and T. J. R. Hughes, *Mathematical Foundations of Elasticity*.
- A. C. Eringen, *Microcontinuum Field Theories*.
- E. and F. Cosserat, *Theory of Deformable Bodies*.
- M. O. Katanaev and I. V. Volovich, papers on geometric theory of defects.
- G. K. Batchelor, *An Introduction to Fluid Dynamics*.
- P. G. Drazin and W. H. Reid, *Hydrodynamic Stability*.
- P. J. Morrison, articles on Hamiltonian fluid mechanics.
- V. I. Arnold and B. Khesin, *Topological Methods in Hydrodynamics*.
